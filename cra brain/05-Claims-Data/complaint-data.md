# Florida Insurer Complaint Data

Sources: [FL OIR Stability Report Jan 2026](https://floir.gov/docs-sf/default-source/property-and-casualty/stability-unit-reports/january-2026-isu-report-final.pdf) · [ValuePenguin/NAIC](https://www.valuepenguin.com/most-common-insurance-complaints) · [Newsweek — FL Insurers Fined](https://www.newsweek.com/florida-insurers-fined-millions-mishandling-hurricane-claims-2123563)

## Florida's outsized litigation share

- Florida generated **8–16% of nationwide homeowner claims** from 2016–2023
- Florida was responsible for **64–80% of the nation's homeowner lawsuits** in the same period

This extreme ratio is why [[03-Florida-Law/sb-2a-2022|SB 2-A]] and [[03-Florida-Law/hb-837-2023|HB 837]] were passed.

## NAIC complaint surge

Homeowners hazard insurance complaints surged **4,866%** from 2021 to 2024 (NAIC data).

## When complaints are investigated, consumers usually win

| Outcome | % of investigated complaints |
|---------|------------------------------|
| Company position overturned | **26.2%** |
| Compromised settlement reached | 26.1% |
| Claim settled | 18.7% |
| Company upheld | **only 4.1%** |

## Hurricane claims misconduct

After Hurricanes Ian and Idalia, Florida OIR examined insurers and found:
- **8 of 10 carriers examined** guilty of claims-handling misconduct after Idalia
- Error rates as high as **60-80%** at some carriers
- Combined fines: **$2.075 million** assessed in 2025
