# OPPAGA Study — The Definitive Proof of Public Adjuster Value

## Source

**OPPAGA Report 10-06** — Florida Office of Program Policy Analysis & Government Accountability

- Report page: https://oppaga.fl.gov/Products/ReportDetail?rn=10-06
- Full PDF: http://www.oppaga.state.fl.us/reports/pdf/1006rpt.pdf
- Critical analysis: https://johnsonstrategiesllc.com/public-adjusters-747-bogus

Dataset: 76,321 Citizens Property Insurance claims from 2008-2009 study, covering 2004-2005 hurricanes.

## Key findings

| Scenario | With PA | Without PA | Difference |
|----------|---------|-----------|------------|
| 2005 hurricane claims | **$17,187 avg** | $2,029 avg | **+747%** |
| Non-catastrophe claims | **$9,379 avg** | $1,391 avg | **+574%** |
| 2004 hurricane claims | **$22,266 avg** | $18,659 avg | **+~19%** |

## Critical context — read before using these numbers

The **747% figure** is real but has important limitations:
1. Applies specifically to **Citizens Insurance 2005 hurricane claims** — not all claims
2. Fee caps (20% standard, 10% emergency) **did not exist** during this study period (2004-2005)
3. The 2004 data shows a more modest **19% advantage** — not 747%
4. The study is from **2008-2009** — market and regulations have changed significantly

**Bottom line:** The data consistently shows PA representation produces materially higher settlements. The 574% and 747% figures are accurate for their specific context. CRA should use them with appropriate framing ("in one study of Citizens Insurance hurricane claims...") rather than as universal claims.

## How competitors use this data

[[02-Competitors/peoples-choice-public-adjusters|People's Choice PA]] prominently cites 574%/747% on their website. This data is essentially an industry-standard marketing statistic.

## Recommended usage for CRA

- Use as supporting evidence alongside CRA's own case results ($18K → $147K)
- Frame correctly: "Studies show PA-represented claims result in settlements 574% higher on average"
- Pair with CRA's real case studies for credibility
