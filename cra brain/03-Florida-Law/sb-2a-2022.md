# SB 2-A — Florida Insurance Reform (December 2022)

## Overview

Signed **December 16, 2022** — the most sweeping Florida property insurance reform in a generation.

Source: [Clyde & Co Analysis](https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2023/03/historic-florida-insurance-reforms-under-sb-2-a) · [FL CFO Summary](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/ica/propertyinsurancechanges)

## Key provisions

### Eliminated one-way attorney fees
Previously, policyholders who won property insurance lawsuits had their attorney fees paid by the insurer. SB 2-A eliminated this, dramatically reducing litigation incentive.

### Banned Assignment of Benefits (AOB)
**Effective January 1, 2023** — residential and commercial property policies can no longer assign benefits to third parties (typically contractors). This was a major driver of litigation fraud.

### Shortened claim filing deadlines

| Claim type | Old deadline | New deadline |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| New claims | 2 years | **1 year** |
| Reopened claims | 2 years | **1 year** |
| Supplemental claims | 3 years | **18 months** |

### Accelerated insurer response timelines

| Action | Old timeline | New timeline |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Acknowledge claim | 14 days | **7 days** |
| Inspect property | 45 days | **30 days** |
| Pay or deny | 90 days | **60 days** |

### Bad faith reform
Bad faith claims now require **adverse adjudication by a court** before a bad faith action can be filed. Previously, a simple denial could trigger bad faith.

### FORA reinsurance backstop
Created **$1 billion Florida Optional Reinsurance Assistance (FORA)** program for the 2023 hurricane season.

## Relevance to CRA

1. **Shorter claim deadlines are urgent** — policyholders who don't act quickly may forfeit rights. CRA's marketing should emphasize deadline urgency.
2. AOB ban redirects contractor relationships — referral networks need to be purely organic
3. Faster insurer timelines mean CRA must be prepared to move quickly
