# Florida Insurance Crisis

## Overview

Florida's property insurance market is one of the most volatile in the US — shaped by hurricane exposure, carrier insolvencies, regulatory reform, and ongoing legislative changes. Signs of stabilization are emerging in 2024-2025.

## Carrier insolvencies (2021–2023)

Seven carriers went insolvent 2021-2022:
1. Gulfstream Property & Casualty
2. St. Johns Insurance
3. Lighthouse Property Insurance
4. Avatar Property & Casualty
5. Southern Fidelity Insurance
6. Weston Property & Casualty
7. FedNat Insurance

**United Property & Casualty** followed in **February 2023**.

Source: [Insurance.com — FL Carrier Exits](https://www.insurance.com/home-and-renters-insurance/home-insurers-leaving-florida)

## Major carriers that exited or restricted

- **State Farm** — stopped writing new policies
- **Farmers Insurance** — stopped writing new policies
- **AIG/Lexington** — exited Florida
- **Allstate** — exited Florida
- **Lemonade** — exited Florida

## Premium impact

Florida homeowners pay approximately **148% above the national average** — the highest premiums in the U.S.

## Signs of stabilization (2024–2025)

| Indicator | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Citizens Policy Count | Peaked at **1.4 million** (2023) → ~**385,000** by late 2025 |
| Underwriting profit | First since 2015, recorded in **2024** |
| Citizens rate change | **8.7% average decrease** — largest in 24 years |
| OIR rate decrease filings | **73 carriers** filed for decreases |
| New market entrants | **16 new insurers** entered Florida |

Source: [Insurance Journal Dec 2025](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/southeast/2025/12/11/850645.htm) · [FL OIR Stability Report Jan 2026](https://floir.gov/docs-sf/default-source/property-and-casualty/stability-unit-reports/january-2026-isu-report-final.pdf)

## Relevance to CRA

- The crisis era (2021-2023) created a huge wave of underpaid/denied claims that PA firms benefited from
- Stabilization doesn't eliminate the need for PAs — insurers still underpay; new entrants may have higher error rates
- The claim deadline changes ([[03-Florida-Law/sb-2a-2022|SB 2-A]]) mean older post-storm claims may be expiring
