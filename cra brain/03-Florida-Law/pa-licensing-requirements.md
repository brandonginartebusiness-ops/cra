# Florida Public Adjuster Licensing Requirements

## License type

**3-20 Public Adjuster License** — issued by Florida Department of Financial Services (DFS)

Source: [FL DFS Licensing](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters)

## Eligibility requirements

- 18+ years old
- U.S. citizen or authorized worker
- High school diploma
- Fingerprinting required
- **$50,000 surety bond** required
- Pass state exam
- Prior experience: licensed all-lines adjuster (6-20) OR 6 months as PA apprentice (31-20)

Sources: [FL DFS](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters) · [SuretyBonds.com Guide](https://www.suretybonds.com/guide/florida/public-adjuster-license)

## Continuing education

**24 hours every 2 years**
- 4 hours: Law & Ethics (mandatory)
- 20 hours: Elective

## Fee caps — FL Statute §626.854(11)

| Situation | Maximum Fee |
|-----------|-------------|
| Standard claims | **20%** of settlement |
| Declared emergency period (1 year after declaration) | **10%** |
| Insurer pays policy limits within 14 days | **1%** |
| Amounts paid before contract date | **0%** |

Source: [FL Statute §626.854](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2025/626.854)

## Solicitation rules

- Allowed: Monday–Saturday, 8 a.m.–8 p.m.
- **48-hour waiting period** after a disaster event before initiating client contact
- Cannot cold-call during restricted hours or in restricted timeframes

## Contract requirements

- **18-point bold type** cancellation notice required in contract
- Client has **10-day cancellation period** (30 days during declared emergencies)
- PA must provide written itemized estimate within **60 days**

## Violations and penalties

- Up to **$10,000 per violation** ($20,000 during emergencies)
- PAs are **strictly prohibited** from paying referral compensation to non-PAs — see [[03-Florida-Law/referral-compensation-rules|Referral Compensation Rules]]
