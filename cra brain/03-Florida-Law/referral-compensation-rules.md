# Referral Compensation Rules — FL §626.854(12-13)

## The rule

**Florida Statute §626.854(12-13) strictly prohibits** public adjusters from compensating any non-PA for referrals.

Source: [FL Statute §626.854](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2025/626.854) · [Florida Bar Ethics Note](https://www.floridabar.org/the-florida-bar-news/lawyers-cant-guarantee-public-adjusters-fee-in-exchange-for-referral/)

## What is PROHIBITED

- Paid referral fees to **contractors**
- Paid referral fees to **real estate agents**
- Paid referral fees to **attorneys**
- Paid referral fees to **any non-PA source**

## Penalties

- **$10,000 per violation**
- **$20,000 per violation** during declared emergencies

## What IS allowed

- PA-to-PA referral fees
- Organic relationship building (without compensation agreements)
- Attorney co-marketing partnerships (no fee sharing)
- Community networking
- Being recommended organically by professionals who trust CRA's work

## Legal referral building strategies

Build organic relationships through:
- [[06-Trust-Authority/fapia-membership|FAPIA]] conferences and networking events
- Legal and real estate community events
- Co-educational workshops with attorneys (both parties educate, no money changes hands)
- Being the PA local professionals recommend organically because of demonstrated results

## Relevance to CRA

This is a critical compliance issue. CRA must not offer contractor co-marketing deals, referral fees to water mitigation companies, or compensation to any non-PA referral source — regardless of how informally it's structured.
