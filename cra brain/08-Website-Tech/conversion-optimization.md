# Conversion Optimization

Sources: [Group107 CRO](https://group107.com/blog/conversion-rate-optimization-best-practices/) · [Unbounce B2B Conversion Rates](https://unbounce.com/conversion-rate-optimization/b2b-conversion-rates/)

## CTA fundamentals

| Element | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| Position | Primary CTA **above the fold** on every page |
| Color | Contrasting color (not matching the background) |
| Language | First person: "**Start my free claim review**" (not "Get a free review") |
| Impact | First-person CTAs increase clicks by up to **90%** |

## Forms

**Multi-step forms** vs. single-step:
- Multi-step forms have shown up to **500% conversion increase** over single-step
- Structure: Damage type (dropdown) → Contact info → Property details

**Minimum required fields:**
1. Name
2. Phone
3. Email
4. Damage Type (dropdown: Hurricane, Water, Roof, Fire/Smoke, Mold, Other)
5. Brief description (optional but helpful)

## Trust signals adjacent to every CTA

Place these immediately next to every form and CTA button:
- FL License W549958
- FAPIA member badge (once joined)
- Google star rating
- "No Recovery, No Fee"
- BBB seal (once accredited)

## Sticky elements

- **Sticky header** on desktop: includes phone number and CTA button
- **Sticky mobile footer bar** with tap-to-call button — mobile conversions depend on this

## Exit-intent popup

Offer downloadable resource when visitor is about to leave:
- "Insurance Claim Checklist" PDF
- "Florida Homeowner's Guide to Filing a Claim"
- Capture email in exchange

## Urgency messaging (ethical)

Use real urgency — do not fabricate:
- "Florida law sets strict filing deadlines — don't wait" (true per [[03-Florida-Law/sb-2a-2022|SB 2-A]] 1-year window)
- "Storm claims must be filed within 1 year" (factual)
- "Free consultation — available today"
