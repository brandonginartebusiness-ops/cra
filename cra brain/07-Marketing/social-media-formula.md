# Social Media Content Formula

## 40/30/20/10 Content Split

| % | Type | Examples |
|---|------|---------|
| **40%** | Educational | Insurance tips, policy explainers, homeowner rights, "did you know" facts |
| **30%** | Social proof | Before/after photos, settlement amounts, video testimonials, Google review screenshots |
| **20%** | Community/brand | Team photos, community events, storm response footage, behind-the-scenes |
| **10%** | Promotional | Free consultation offers, service announcements, deadline urgency |

## Posting frequency

**Minimum: 3x per week** across primary platforms (Facebook, Instagram)

## Hashtag strategy

**Primary:**
`#PublicAdjuster` `#InsuranceClaim` `#FloridaPublicAdjuster` `#ClaimRemedyAdjusters`

**Location tags:**
`#Miami` `#SouthFlorida` `#MiamiLakes` `#FloridaHomeowners`

**Damage-specific:**
`#WaterDamage` `#HurricaneDamage` `#RoofDamage` `#InsuranceHelp`

## Platform priorities

| Platform | Priority | Action |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Facebook | High — fix first | Get to 100+ likes, post 3x/week |
| Instagram | High | Already active — increase frequency |
| LinkedIn | Add ASAP | Company page — missing entirely |
| YouTube | Add within 60 days | Video testimonials and educational content |
| TikTok | Consider | Short-form storm and claims content for younger homeowners |

## Storm response protocol

When a named storm threatens or hits Florida:
1. Immediately post storm preparation content
2. Post safety check-in during/after
3. Post "we're here to help" with CTA
4. Surge ad budget (see [[07-Marketing/google-ads|Google Ads]])
5. Document real damage assessments for ongoing content
