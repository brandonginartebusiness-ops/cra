# Google Ads Strategy

## Industry CPC landscape

Insurance industry keywords carry some of the **highest CPCs in Google Ads**:

| Keyword type | Estimated CPC |
|-------------|--------------|
| Public adjuster terms | ~$15–50+ per click |
| Insurance claim terms | $10–40+ per click |

High CPC = qualified, high-intent traffic. The math works if conversion rate and average claim value are strong.

## Recommended campaign structure

### Google Call Campaigns
Primary format for a PA firm — people dealing with property damage want to call, not fill out a form.

### Keyword targets

**Primary targeting:**
- "public adjuster near me"
- "insurance claim help Florida"
- "[damage type] claim help" (water, hurricane, roof, fire)

**Match types:** Exact match + phrase match (avoid broad match for high-CPC terms)

### Negative keywords (add from the start)
- "jobs"
- "salary"
- "how to become"
- "license exam"
- "school"
- "course"
- "study guide"

### Geographic targeting
- Miami-Dade County (primary)
- Broward County
- Palm Beach County
- Expand to additional FL counties as budget allows

## Seasonal strategy

**Storm surge budgets:** Dramatically increase budget **during and immediately after** named storm events. This is when homeowners have maximum urgency and are actively searching.

**Off-season:** Reduce budget, focus spend on water damage and evergreen terms.

## Landing pages

Each campaign should link to a **dedicated landing page** (not the homepage):
- Hurricane damage campaign → `/services/hurricane-wind-damage/`
- Water damage campaign → `/services/water-damage/`
- See [[04-SEO-Strategy/site-architecture|Site Architecture]] for full page map

## Timeline

Launch Google Ads at **Day 61-90** of the 90-day sprint — after service pages, trust signals, and the review campaign are in place. Google Ads before a strong trust foundation wastes budget.
