The Basics

A public adjuster is a licensed professional who represents homeowners — not insurance companies — during the insurance claim process. Our role is to inspect your damage, prepare thorough claim documentation, and negotiate on your behalf to ensure you receive a fair settlement under your policy.
The insurance company's adjuster works for your insurer and is motivated to minimize your payout. A public adjuster works exclusively for you and is motivated to maximize it. Having professional representation on your side levels the playing field significantly.
You can file a claim yourself — but research consistently shows that policyholders represented by public adjusters receive significantly higher settlements. Insurance policies are complex, damage scopes are easy to underestimate, and negotiation is a skill. Most homeowners who go it alone leave substantial money behind without knowing it.
Yes. We are fully licensed Florida public adjusters, regulated by the Florida Department of Financial Services. We adhere to all state standards and ethical guidelines for public adjuster practice.

The Process

We start with a free, no-obligation claim review. We'll discuss your situation, review your policy basics, and give you an honest assessment of your options. If we move forward, we'll schedule an on-site inspection at your property.
Our licensed adjusters conduct a thorough on-site inspection of all affected areas. We photograph and document everything — visible and hidden damage — and take measurements to build an accurate, itemized scope of loss. We look for what the insurance company's adjuster typically misses.
It depends on the complexity of your claim and how quickly your insurer responds. Straightforward claims can resolve in a few weeks. More complex claims — especially those involving disputes, scope negotiations, or supplemental documentation — can take several months. We keep you informed throughout and push the process forward consistently.
The most helpful documents are your insurance policy (declarations page and full policy), any existing claim correspondence from your insurer, photos or videos of the damage you may have taken, and any estimates or prior inspection reports. We'll guide you on exactly what's needed at each stage.
Emergency or temporary repairs to prevent further damage are fine — and often required under your policy. However, don't make permanent repairs or dispose of damaged materials before we document the full scope. Evidence is critical to your claim.

Fees & Cost

We work on a contingency fee basis — meaning we only get paid if you do. Our fee is a percentage of your final settlement, typically in the range of 10–20% depending on the complexity of the claim. Florida law caps public adjuster fees for certain claim types. We'll discuss all of this clearly before you sign anything.
No. There is zero upfront cost to work with us. The free claim review, the inspection, and all claim preparation are included. You only pay us after your claim is settled and you've received your payout.
Our contingency structure ensures we're only financially successful when you are. In practice, the settlements we negotiate almost always result in substantially more money for the homeowner — even after our fee. We're transparent about this from the start and will give you an honest assessment of your situation before we proceed.
